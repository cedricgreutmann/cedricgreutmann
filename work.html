3D/VR/Video Game

Historic Lens: Edinburgh VR

About


In this project I wanted to explore and create a VR experience. I recreated a historic map of Edinburgh in 3D space that can be explored with a VR headset. Its main feature is that it shows how the city has grown through three different time periods. This experience is based off a map found in the National Library archives. The map consisted is a combination of different time period maps, with color coded streets and buildings to show when certain areas were built.

I used this map and traced abstract 3D models off of it. To create the basic layout of the city. To add to the map I then created more detailed models of certain most iconic buildings, which, in VR, can be picked up and investigated closer.

The 3D assets were created in Maya 2016, and textured in Substance Painter. I finally took the models and programmed all the VR experience in the Unity game engine.

Roles


3D Modeller and texturing (Maya and Substance Painter), Unity Engine programing

Visual Design

Concept Currency

About


Here I was creating a new design concept for a future world, layed out in a design brief, that had been flooded by effects of global warming. This fictional currency named the: Regulated Dollar, would be the world wide currency. Therefore the design and concept of the currency had to appeal to the world population.

These designs included notes for 50, 100, 500 and 1000. With each of the notes representing a theme. For example the 50, was to represent world peace. Since countries in this fictional world had to overcome this flooding together they now would have to stick together as one. The other notes represent renewable energy, Asian world wonders, and finally European world wonders. The historical buildings notes, so the last two, were supposed to represent potentially lost monumental buildings after the flooding.

These notes were mainly created with Photoshop, using images and fractals gathered from the web, then blended together. The symbols were created in Illustrator.

Roles


Designer, created with Illustrator and Photoshop
