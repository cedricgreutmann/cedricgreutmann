Where to Next? - Navigation through Environmental Cues in a Virtual Environemnt

About

This dissertation project was the final piece to completing my Msc in Design and Digital media. I wanted to explore if it was possible to direct players of a video game through environment cues. To do this I created a interior level, in a underground bunker. This maze like tunnel system was supposed to be very confusing and disorientating. Through this level at certain points I had intersections where I purposly placed one of a few environmental cues. These were meant to either direct a player down the correct path, or deter them the players from the incorrect paths to the correct one. Once I had this level built I had players test the level to see what out of the cues I placed into the level actually work and which did not.

Overall the experience was very interesting as it was interesting to see the influence the different envirornmtal cues had on the players. Especially when looking at the background of the players with previous experiences with games and if that influenced them.

Roles

Modeling, texturing of all assets in Maya and Substance Painter. Assembling modular assets into level in Unreal Engine. Integrating small interactions for players as well as player controls.

Starfish Novel - Beebe Station

About

In this project we built a game level of a under water station, based of the novel Starfish (2000) by Peter Watts. This is a modular level, meaning the components of the base are seperate and can be easily modified to create new areas. Each person created their own models, but sharing of the assets was permitted in the second submission(making sure to give credit where due).
From the reading I imagined the base to be very small and crammed. Giving the player a bit of a claustraphobic feeling when exploring. The idea behind the project was to only create a 3D space that could be explored. However while desiging the level, I realized I was missing something a gameplay mechanic. So I added a simple mechanic of finding the memory tapes throughout the station. Similar to other games of finding collectables.
Once the player had found the tapes could they pass the level and continue on to a potential future level.For this project I also worked with a other student in the sound program, to create the sounds the station gives off, as well as the recordings of the voice on the tapes spread across the station.

Roles

Unity Engine programming, 3D models and texturing. Sound design work by Gabrielle Haley.

Historic Lens: Edinburgh VR

About

In this project I wanted to create a VR experience. I recreated a historic map of Edinburgh in 3D space that can be explored with a Vive VR headset. Its main feature is that it shows how the city has grown through three different time periods. This experience is based off a map found in the National Library archives. The map consisted of a combination of different time period maps, with color coded streets and buildings to show when certain areas were built.

I used this map and traced abstract 3D models off of it. To create the basic layout of the city. To add to the map I then created more detailed models of certain more iconic buildings, which in VR, can be picked up using the vive controllers and investigated closer.

The 3D assets were created in Maya 2016, and textured in Substance Painter. I finally took the models and programmed all the VR experience in the Unity game engine.

Roles

3D Modeling/texturing (Maya and Substance Painter), Unity Engine programing.
